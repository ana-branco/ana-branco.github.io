anniisart
the brand
This is the brand I dedicated to my illustration and drawing projects. Back in 2019, I began to regularly post them in my Instagram page, and soon after, I started to make stickers, prints, pins, bookmarks and other kinds of stationary with my art and selling it. I have been able to keep my little shop just on Instagram, but I intend to make myself a website, and this is the one I designed.
communication
I am very casual and relaxed when speaking with my clients, and I enjoy building a good relationship with my audience, sharing daily sketches and parts of my art process.
The icon with a cartoon-like version of my face works like a logo in this case, giving a friendly face to the brand right away, that displays immediately the cartoon and anime inspired style of my illustrations. For matters of identification and credits/rights, there's a watermark with my initials AB, signed on the illustrations/pieces.
colors
The mustard yellow, white and the light pink have been usual colors for this brand since the beginning, so I decided to keep them. I needed a more muted color to go off against in the website, especially as to not grab the attention away from the illustrations, which is why I picked the dark blue.
art direction
Because it's a website that displays illustrations and art, I wanted to keep the rest of the interface simple and clean as to not distract from them, and just be a good compliment. These illustrations are vibrant and colorful, done in a cartoon and anime inspired style.

desktop experience

homepage
The hero shows up with just the illustration by itself at first, but as soon as you scroll down a little, it shows an introductory paragraph about the me, with two buttons that will lead to the shop or to the art page.
navigation
After scrolling past the hero, the navigation bar, that had the illustration as background, now shows up in front of the dark blue solid background. This is how it will be showing throughout the rest of the website, on all of the other pages.
art
For the art page I designed a grid, that allows the viewer to see many pieces at once. From that, you can click one particular piece, to go to that piece's page and find out more about it.
illustration page
In each illustration page, there's a feature at the bottom that allows the viewer to toggle between the final version and the sketch, allowing a more in depth look at the artist's process. Above this button, there is also information about the total time spent, materials/ software, and a small text explaining the drawing.
shop
The shop displays the articles in an organized grid, each one of them having their name and price on top. By hovering, this information disappears, letting the item show up clearly. By clicking an article, that item's page will open.
item page
In this page, you will see all the information pertaining to the product - materials, size and price. Below, there is the button to add to the shopping cart.

mobile experience

hero and menu
The whole mobile version is very similar to the desktop one. The navigation mar on top becomes a burger menu that opens up to the whole page.
shop
In the homepage, below the hero and the Instagram call there is an announcement to the shop. The shop itself is displayed in a scroll down, instead of the desktop grid, and the overlay with the item name and price is smaller, covering just a strip at the bottom, for easier mobile visualization.
item page
It's very similar to the desktop version, with all the necessary info on the product, and the button to add it to the shopping cart, as well as a button to go back to the shop page.
